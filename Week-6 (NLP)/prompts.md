# Prompts for Learning NLP with Hugging Face Transformers

This file contains a collection of prompts that can be used to explore and learn about Natural Language Processing (NLP) with the Hugging Face Transformers library. These prompts are designed to guide newcomers in understanding the concepts and functionalities of the library, as well as to provide opportunities for experimentation and exploration.

## Project-Specific Prompts

- **Sentiment Analysis:**

  - "Analyze the sentiment of the following sentence: 'I love using the Hugging Face Transformers library!'"
  - "Classify the sentiment of this movie review: '[Insert movie review text here]'"

- **Text Generation:**

  - "Generate a creative story based on the prompt: 'A lone astronaut lands on a mysterious planet.'"
  - "Write a poem in the style of Shakespeare about the beauty of nature."

- **Question Answering:**

  - "Answer the question: 'What is the capital of France?' based on the following context: '[Insert context about France here]'"
  - "Find the answer to the question: 'How does a car engine work?' using this text: '[Insert text about car engines here]'"

- **Text Summarization:**

  - "Summarize the following news article: '[Insert news article text here]'"
  - "Provide a concise summary of this scientific paper: '[Insert scientific paper text here]'"

## Hugging Face Transformers Module-Specific Prompts

- **Model Exploration:**

  - "Describe the architecture of the BERT model."
  - "Explain the differences between GPT-2 and GPT-3."

- **Pipeline Usage:**

  - "How can I use the `pipeline` function for sentiment analysis?"
  - "Demonstrate how to generate text using the `text-generation` pipeline."

- **Tokenization:**

  - "What is tokenization and why is it important in NLP?"
  - "Show an example of tokenizing a sentence using the Hugging Face tokenizer."

## Other Relevant Prompts

- **NLP Concepts:**

  - "Explain the difference between Natural Language Understanding (NLU) and Natural Language Generation (NLG)."
  - "What are the common applications of NLP in real-world scenarios?"

- **LLM Exploration:**

  - "What are Large Language Models (LLMs) and how do they work?"
  - "Describe the capabilities and limitations of LLMs in NLP tasks."

## Tips for Using Prompts

- Be clear and specific in your prompts.
- Provide sufficient context for the LLM to understand your request.
- Experiment with different prompt variations to see how the LLM responds.
- Use the LLM's output as a starting point for your own learning and exploration.
